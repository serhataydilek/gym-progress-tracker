1. Research: Study existing fitness apps to understand their features, strengths, and weaknesses.
2. Define user requirements: Identify the key functionalities your target audience wants in a gym progress
tracking app.
3. Design UI/UX: Create a user-friendly interface that' easy to navigate and visually appealing.
4. Develop back-end: Implement server-side logic for data storage and retrieval.
5. Integrate APIs: Connect with fitness data APIs such as Google Fit or Apple HealthKit to track users' exercise
progress.
6. Exercise database: Create a comprehensive database of exercises, including sets, reps, and weight information.
7. Input & tracking: Allow users to input their chosen exercise along with the desired set, rep, and weight
values, then track their progress over time.
8. Calendar/Planner: Integrate a calendar or planner function that helps users schedule their workout routine
based on the exercises they've added.
9. Test: Conduct thorough testing to ensure the app functions correctly, is bug-free, and meets user requirements.
10. Launch: Release your app on the desired platforms (iOS, Android) and promote it through various channels.
11. Maintain: Regularly update the app based on user feedback and market trends to keep it relevant and popular.
